⚠ Failure to comply with the IVAO Rules and Regulations (R&R), the XE Individual Tour Rules (XE.ITR) for each specific tour, and the XE General Tour Rules (XE.GTR) will result in your report being rejected.

§ XE.GTR.1 - Online, Real Time, Real Weather

Flights shall be conducted online on IVAO, in real time mode (1x simulation rate), and using real weather, except under any of the following circumstances:

  1. Online: Forced offline after becoming airborne due to a server-side technical issue (e.g., the World Server is down),
  2. Online: Forced offline due to a client-side technical issue (e.g., simulator crash, or temporary network outage), a maximum disconnection period of 20 minutes may be permitted. Upon reconnecting, the flight shall be resumed no farther than 50 NM from the point of disconnection.

Pilots shall include the following note in the Remarks field of the report: [OFFLINE] {xx:xx ~ xx:xx}: {briefly describe the situation}.

⚠ Caution
  • Online: Pilots are recommended to check their flight status on Webeye to ensure that the flight record is valid.
⛔ Forbidden
  • Real-Time: Pilots are prohibited from using pause, slew, or acceleration modes.
  • Real Weather: Pilots are prohibited from using non-realistic wind conditions to gain a tailwind advantage or to disregard a headwind disadvantage.

§ XE.GTR.2 - Flight Order

Flights shall be completed in the correct sequence.

§ XE.GTR.3 - Valid Flight Plan

Pilots shall submit a valid flight plan before pushback. A valid flight plan includes, but is not limited to, the following:

  1. A correct combination of the ICAO airline designator and flight number, used as the callsign, if applicable, in Item 7.
    ⚠ Caution
    • All tours in the Life of an Airline Pilot series require this. Refer to the Individual Tour Rules for details.
  2. Correct flight rules, in Item 8.
  3. A suitable aircraft type, properly equipped to operate safely and within limits for the intended flight, in Item 9.
    ⛔ Forbidden
    • Pilots are prohibited from refueling in mid-air or making a stopover for refueling.
  4. Correct departure and destination aerodromes, plus at least 1 suitable alternate aerodrome, in Items 13 and 16.
    ⛔ Forbidden
    • The alternate aerodrome shall not be the same as the departure aerodrome.
  5. A valid cruising level and route, in Item 15.
    ⚠ Caution
    • If at or below the transition altitude, the format shall be Axxx. If at or above the transition level, the format shall be Fxxx.
    • The metric system is used in the PRC.
    • Pilots shall not include SID and STAR in the route.
    ⛔ Forbidden
    • Routes with DCT, DCT/GPS, or VFR DCT will not be accepted.
  6. Necessary information, if applicable, in Item 18.
    ⚠ Caution
    • Most tours require a tour remark. Refer to the Individual Tour Rules for details.
    • Use a space (“ ”) to separate multiple entries in RMK/. Example: RMK/WORLDTOUR TCAS SIMBRIEF.

§ XE.GTR.4 - 3-Minutes Regulation

After connecting to, or before disconnecting from, the network, the aircraft shall remain stationary at the apron or gate for at least 3 minutes.

§ XE.GTR.5 - Charts and NavData

Pilots shall have the latest charts and AIRAC information available on board.

§ XE.GTR.6 - Procedures

Pilots shall comply with the general procedures, including but not limited to:

  1. The maximum speed below FL100 or A100 is 250 KIAS, unless otherwise instructed by ATC.
  2. The maximum altitude is FL410, unless otherwise instructed by ATC.
    ⚠ Caution
    • Due to possible altitude discrepancies between a pilot’s simulator and IVAO’s tracking system, the maximum recorded altitude (Max Altitude in the Leg List) is set to 42,000 ft.
  3. Pilots shall comply with all altitude and speed restrictions published on the charts, unless otherwise instructed by ATC.
  4. Pilots shall contact the appropriate ATC units along their flight path and strictly comply with their instructions. Otherwise, they shall monitor and broadcast their intentions on UNICOM (122.800 MHz).

§ XE.GTR.7 - Leg Report

Pilots shall submit the report within 7 days after completing the flight.

⚠ Caution
  • Although the tour system may auto-fill data, pilots shall verify the recorded information in IVAO Tracker.

§ XE.GTR.8 - Next Flight

Pilots shall disconnect from the network before starting the next flight.

§ XE.GTR.9 - Diversion

Pilots may divert to an alternate aerodrome under any of the following circumstances:

  1. Upon arrival over the destination aerodrome, if multiple unsuccessful approaches, extended holding, or the anticipation thereof cause the aircraft’s fuel to fall below the required minimum, a diversion becomes necessary.

Pilots shall:

  1. Conduct no more than 1 diversion per leg.
  2. Complete the continuation flight from the alternate aerodrome to the destination aerodrome within 24 hours after the first flight, using the same callsign and aircraft.
  3. After arriving at the destination aerodrome, submit the report with the following criteria:
    1. Date: Enter the date of the first flight.
    2. Callsign: Enter the same callsign as the first flight.
    3. Take Off Time: Enter the time of the first flight.
    4. Landing Time: Enter the time of the second flight.
    5. Remarks: Include the following note: [DIVERSION] {alternate ICAO}: {detailed description of the situation and rationale}.
ℹ️ Note
  • Note that most other divisions do not accept diversions.

§ XE.GTR.10 - Report Modification

Validators may, at their discretion, return the report for modification, with or without issuing a warning, if minor mistakes are identified. Such a warning may be issued only once within a single tour; any subsequent occurrence will result in the report being rejected.

ℹ️ Note
  • Message [WARNING] {reason} will be included in the validation comment.

§ XE.GTR.11 - Rules and Regulations

Pilots shall comply with the following rules and regulations, in order of precedence:

  1. IVAO Rules and Regulations (R&R).
  2. XE Individual Tour Rules (XE.ITR) for each specific tour.
  3. XE General Tour Rules (XE.GTR).

Pilots repeatedly failing to comply with these rules or regulations will be permanently banned from participating in XE tours.

§ XE.GTR.12 - Amendment and Interpretation Rights

The XE Events Department reserves the right to amend these General Tour Rules (GTR) and holds the final right of interpretation.

Should you have any inquiries, please contact the XE Events Department via email at xe-events@ivao.aero, or via the XE Discord server.
